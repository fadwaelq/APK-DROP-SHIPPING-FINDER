Smudging, the ritual burning of sage, is done to clear a space or person of negative energies. White sage (Salvia apiana), also known as sacred sage, is an evergreen perennial native to the Southwestern United States. This potent herb has been used for centuries by Native Americans in rituals of cleansing and healing. Many people continue this tradition today by holding their own sage smudging ceremonies. Burn white sage to clear energy and restore balance, for protection and spiritual purification. Lavender flowers/buds come from the plant family Lamiaceae (the mint family) and have a wonderful variety of uses. These buds are pleasantly aromatic and maintain full aroma when dried. Our Sage and lavender is grown without the use of pesticides! It is 100% natural Sage and lavender, bundled together using a 100% cotton string. Each stick is approximately 4 inches long and 1 inch wide.As this is a natural product please expect some variation. Burning Instructions: Hold the incense wand over an open flame, tilting the end down at 45-degree angle. Once lit, let the stick burn visibly for approximately 20 seconds, relighting as necessary.Blow out the flame and allow it to smolder. If the stick is difficult to light we recommend untying the string and unraveling it a little bit. Sometimes the bundle is tight and doesn’t leave much room for oxygen to ignite the flame.
