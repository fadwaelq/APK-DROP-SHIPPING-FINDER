Analog video cable used for transmitting video signals between a computer or other video source and a display device, such as a monitor or projector. SVGA cables are backward-compatible with VGA connections, which means you can use an SVGA cable with a VGA device without any issues. Compared to digital video cables, such as DVI or HDMI, SVGA cables are generally more cost-effective, which makes them a popular choice for older devices or budget-conscious setups.
